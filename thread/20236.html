Details of the Final Tournament of the Gomoku World Championship (GAT)
Tournament system: 12 players Round Robin (11 rounds)
Time control: 135 minutes per player + 30 seconds added for each move

Opening rule: Swap 2

The 2013 GAT will be the first event of Gomoku World Championship history where players get a financial prize.
The best 3 of the 12 contenders will win the the prizes as follows:

1st place – 600
2nd place – 300
3rd place – 150

The numbers may rise if we found more supporters.
The winner of the tournament will get the title World Champion.
Before the beginning of the tournament the 12 players have to be decided first. Below is the table that illustrates how the 12 seats are taken.

Seat Description
1 The Winner of last World Championship – Attila Demjan, Hungary
2 National Place for last WC 2nd place – Poland
3 National Place for last WC 3rd place – Poland
4 National Place for last WC 4th place - Czech Republic
5 National Place for last WC 5th place – Poland
6 National Place for last WC 6th place – Hungary
7 The 1st place of Qualification Tournament
8 The 2nd place of Qualification Tournament
9 The 3rd place of Qualification Tournament
10 The 4th place of Qualification Tournament
11 The 5th place of Qualification Tournament
12 Organizing Country or the 6th place of GQT

Comments:

The Winner of last World Championship – the current World Champion – can go directly to the A-final without having to join the National or International Qualification tournaments.
National Places for last WC means that the Seats belong to the nations whose players got those places in last WC.
Organizing Country Seat – it is given to Organizing Country if that country has failed to get any National or Personal Places from previous WC, nor any place from QT.
Cancellations – if Winner of last WC, cancel his/her participation – the personal place is changed to a national place. However if any country owning National Places, cancel their participation – the seat(s) is added to the QT seats group.

Details of the Gomoku International Open Tournament (GBT) 
Tournament system: Swiss system – 9 rounds
Time control: 60 minutes per player + 30 seconds added for each move

Opening Rule: Swap 2

The Gomoku International Open Tournament is played in Swiss System, at the same time as the A-final. This tournament offers no titles, however, there is a benefit for top 3 of the tournament: The top 3 players will earn Personal Places for the next International Qualification Tournament (GQT) 2015. Anyone has the right to play in GBT without any limits. No matter if the player is old or young, beginner or a strong player – everyone is welcome!

注：是欧元标志   GAT,GQT，GBT是指无禁组的比赛，RAT，RQT，RGT是指有禁组的比赛
